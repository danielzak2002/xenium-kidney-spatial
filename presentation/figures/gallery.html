Spatial transcriptomics of kidney B/plasma niches — presentation figures

Each study stands on its own first — QC → native cell typing → observed pattern → reproducibility across many sections — BEFORE any cross-dataset normalization. Harmonization (N) and the cross-context comparison (X) come only after, as deliberate steps. The earned conclusion: a conserved B/plasma scaffold acquires context-specific immune wiring — immunoregulatory (Treg-in / cytotoxic-out) in tumor, absent in non-malignant disease.

X · Cross-context — the earned comparison (across disease settings)

B1★ Aggregate marker overlays — the impact figure ★ hero
One representative ccRCC and one DKD B/plasma aggregate (~190 µm field). Blue underlay = B-core; marker+ cells overlaid (MS4A1, MZB1, FOXP3, CD8A, GZMB) plus a composite B/Treg/CD8 panel. Treg-around & cytotoxic-excluded in ccRCC vs cytotoxic-mixed-in in DKD — visible, not just numeric. [PNG · SVG]
C3★ Burden-corrected differential — THE headline ★ hero
Δlog₂ = log₂(Treg) − log₂(effector-CD8) per aggregate, count-pooled with bootstrap 95% CI. ccRCC +2.60 [+2.32,+2.84] (~6× Treg bias) vs DKD +0.24 [−0.31,+0.72] (~1×, no bias); CIs non-overlapping → the immunoregulatory bias is tumor-specific. Immune to the cytotoxic-burden confound. [PNG · SVG]

Per-section neighborhood z across kidney contexts. Absolute z is dominated by immune↔parenchyma geometry (immune cells aggregate, parenchyma 'avoids'); the biology is in the differentials. NOTE: the ccRCC stroma–immune inversion is provisional (not tile-verified). [PNG · SVG]

  • B-aggregate niches are associational / colocalization, not communication or causation.
