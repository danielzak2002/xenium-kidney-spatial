Xenium kidney pilot — Phase-A summary

Dataset: kidney_RCC_protein · modality: gene + 27-plex protein · 465228 cells · generated 2026-06-05
Data: 10x Genomics Xenium human kidney (CC BY 4.0). Code: MIT.

Scope. Validation of the parametrized R/Seurat loop (load → QC → normalize → cluster → annotate → spatial smoke-test) on the smaller of the two datasets, the small→big gate target. The identical code (R/config.R, 01, 02) re-points at the full multimodal section via XENIUM_DATASET=big.

Pipeline steps

  1. Panel assessment — parse gene_panel.json + cell_feature_matrix.h5; confirm immune/B-cell & plasma markers; enumerate control features.
  2. Load & QC (01_load_qc.R) — lean LoadXenium (centroids only); Xenium-specific QC from cells.parquet: flag (not filter) negative-control / blank-codeword / segmentation-merge cells; remove only zero-count cells.
  3. Normalize & cluster & annotate (02_cluster_annotate.R) — LogNormalize (all panel genes as features), PCA/UMAP, native igraph Leiden, marker DE, provisional marker-score annotation.
  4. Spatial smoke-test — immune-neighbourhood fraction on cell centroids (full spatial statistics are Phase B / squidpy).

3 · Immune / B-cell spatial view

Conclusions & observations

The loop is validated. QC reproduces the vendor metrics exactly (median 70 transcripts / 32 genes per cell), and clustering is biologically coherent on the first pass.
  • Biology tracks the known sample. This renal cell carcinoma (Stage III, T3a N1 MX) section recovers coherent compartments, named here by each cluster's own top markers: tumour (C5orf46, EDN1, CDH2, MUC1, SEMA3C); myeloid (TREM2, HAMP, VSIG4, FCGR3A, LILRB4); endothelium (CLEC14A, CD34, BTNL9, ADGRL4, MCF2L); T cell (LAG3, NKG7, GZMA, PDCD1, GZMK); B cell (MS4A1, CD19, BANK1, TCL1A, CD1C); plasma (MZB1, TENT5C, TNFRSF17, SDC1, DERL3). Immune lineages account for ~58% of provisional labels — consistent with the dataset's immune-rich, infiltrated description.
  • B-cell / plasma biology is resolvable despite CD79B being absent from the panel: distinct B-cell (MS4A1, CD19, BANK1, TCL1A, CD1C) and plasma (MZB1, TENT5C, TNFRSF17, SDC1, DERL3) clusters separate cleanly and localise to discrete foci (B/plasma-signal panel) — a promising substrate for the immune-aggregate / TLS question.
  • The immune-heavy composition is largely real, not only a scorer artefact. The section is a tumour with extensive infiltrate; epithelial (nephron) representation is correspondingly sparse, which also makes tubular cell-typing the weakest part of the provisional (z-scored marker-argmax) labels.
  • The 27-plex protein assay is loaded as a separate, CLR-normalized assay and excluded from clustering — available for orthogonal validation of these RNA-defined types (e.g. CD20 / CD3E / CD68 / CD138 protein) without confounding the gene-based clustering.
  • QC flags are low (mean neg-control fraction 9e-05; 3337 candidate segmentation merges of 465228 cells) — segmentation looks trustworthy for cell-level analysis.
Caveats & next steps.
  • Cell-type labels are provisional (marker argmax). Replace with reference-based label transfer (e.g. a kidney/immune atlas) before any quantitative cell-type claim.
  • The spatial smoke-test is a sanity check only; neighbourhood enrichment, co-occurrence, Moran's I and niche detection are done in Phase B (squidpy / spatialdata).
  • Next: use the CLR-normalized protein assay to cross-check RNA cell types, then Phase-B spatial statistics and immune-aggregate / TLS detection.
